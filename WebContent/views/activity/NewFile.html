SHEILA MONDLANE

Our leader (img: Valter Escrivão)

Meet

“Salut! I’m Sheila Mondlane - the girl behind this magic. I’m a Mozambican ‘99 Leo baby. I have discovered in me a great passion for arts in its various form throughout the years since REPRESENT popped into my life as my entity. I admit that I had no passion for any of my craft when I started off but with the years of getting to learn about it – I have become dependent of the greats plans that come with it and a lover of each of their complexities.”

What do I do right now? What am I known for? 

“I’m a blogger and fashion designer. If I am not mistaken it all started around the same phase in my life – I refer to the idea, the brainstorm not the execution – in Grade 7, I was 12. I thought of the concept of my brand (still think through today) and started building each pillar that would support it on a day like today and actually a further future which I have not reached.”

How did REP come about?

“The blog started off as a random Tumblr page in which I just reposted cool pictures – but only black and white. Then, I had an idea with a friend that we’d start a blog together around a time in which her and other friends in common, in our class had just read ’Adorkable’ by Sara Manning (funny, I only read after this) but that took a wrong turn and a downfall and challenged by my own fear to do things alone – I worked on making it as it is known today. I took and take my time to do research, reach out and then edit texts and media to make that final post. Unfortunately, in 2016, I was on a break due to my Matrics (Grade 12) which was time-consuming and I hated how inconsistent it made me. It was a huge sacrifice and pain for me.”

You said you were a fashion designer, tell us about that.

“As a fashion designer, summarizing, I debuted in the age of 14 given the chance to participate in Mozambique Fashion Week under the category of MFW School.
I was too young before. Since then I have never stopped trying and successfully earning a spot each year. Age has been the issue then money and I am pushing for a day in which I go in with the big dogs and shoot my talent for MFW Young Designers. Interest for fashion? Yes over the years. When I started I actually focused on portraits and tried drawing clothing out of boredom amidst classes in Grade 7. I got a compliment and tada. I started working on it and not only when I started participating in MFW and attending the various workshops, interacting with great designers, living that lifestyle and understanding its complexity did I develop that desire to be a fashion designer. I am one.”

Last but not least …

… with REPRESENT I leave a brand and I live for it - to make it happen but also with REPRESENT, I seek to learn the complexity of several other arts and be inspired because then only does one get to appreciate the fruit of a human’s creativity conveyed from within to others.

I run this.
